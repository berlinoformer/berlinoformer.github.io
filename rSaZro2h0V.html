Stress? 8 herbs that can be added to tea or other beverages

Stress? 8 herbs that can be added to tea or other beverages

Stress and anxiety are now an integral part of most people's daily lives. Affordable, simple and safe, but at the same time effective way to reduce the risk of stress is the right tea.

What is anxiety and stress?

Anxiety and stress are common terms that describe a mobilized and stressed state of the body and mind. Short-term stress is good for the body, because it helps to cope with a large number of tasks, to react immediately. However, prolonged stress, especially if it does not alternate with sufficient rest, causes serious harm to health. The reason is that it triggers a series of biochemical reactions in the body and can lead to hormonal problems or a violation of the immune system.

Anxiety is described as a feeling anxiety, fear, or discomfort. It may be related to a specific upcoming event or situation, or it may be a General feeling of restlessness in life. This condition, which lasts for a long time, is very tedious. Regardless of the cause of stress and anxiety, there are various herbs that help to relax.

Best teas from neuroses and anxiety include mint, chamomile, balm, passionflower, green tea, rose, coffee and ashwagandha.

Mint tea.

This tea contains substances that act as natural muscle relaxants. Mint has anti-inflammatory properties that make it a suitable ingredient in tea that warms and helps you relax.

Chamomile.

Chamomile is another plant that is considered one of the best in terms of its ability to cause muscle relaxation and mild drowsiness. Helps clear your mind of obsessive thoughts and prepare you for night rest.

Lemon balm.

Studies have shown that this type of tea can directly affect the level of stress hormones in the blood. At the same time, it improves mood and gives energy, but it does not strain the nervous system like drinks with caffeine.

Passionflower.

Some of antioxidants and active components of passionflower tea help to suppress stress hormones. This reduces the feeling of unease and anxiety. In addition, it helps to combat insomnia, which is often found in people suffering from chronic stress or neurosis. There is one feature - passionflowers should not be combined with antidepressants, as some side effects may appear.

Green tea.

Green tea, known for its anti-inflammatory properties, is an excellent way to naturally regulate hormone metabolism in the body. Helps to improve mood, it is not only invigorating, but also relaxing.

Rose tea.

Rose petal tea is recommended to be consumed before bedtime. It has been proven to help relax the mind and body. This tea is a wonderful aromatic drink, after which you can get a good night's sleep.

Lavender.

Lavender is popular as a relaxing fragrance. However, the plant can also be used for tea. There are studies that show that lavender tea reduces hormonal fluctuations, especially in women. This is due to the active ingredients in the fragrant small flowers. Lavender is recommended for women who are experiencing stress and anxiety. anxiety during menstruation.

Ashwagandha.

For thousands of years, ashwagandha tea has proven to be a cure for neurosis. It is particularly popular in Ayurvedic medicine and is known as one of the most antioxidant-rich teas. In Ayurveda, it is recommended for toning, insomnia and a variety of inflammation. The roots of the ashwagandha plant, also known as Indian ginseng, are particularly useful and are commonly used for tea.

It is worth Recalling that before adding any plants to tea, you need to consult with a specialist.
