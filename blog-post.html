Alcoholism and mental health

Alcoholism and mental health

People who abuse alcohol have changes in the brain that negatively affect their emotional and mental health. A very delicate balance is maintained in the human brain various chemical substances. Under the influence of alcohol, the balance is disturbed and various deviations occur as a result.

Almost all of us have experienced the calming effect that an alcoholic drink has on our body. It is associated with a small change in the very chemical balance in our brain. But with what the more you drink, the more this chemical balance changes, which negatively affects the structure of the brain.

People who regularly drink even moderate amounts of alcohol can begin to experience nervousness, melancholy, anxiety, irritability, and problems with their partner. Insomnia, paranoia and hallucinations are observed with regular alcohol abuse.

Other commonly observed alcohol-related effects on people's mental health are listed below.

Depression.

The phrase "get Drunk to forget" comes from the fact that many people try to suppress their emotions and sadness with alcohol. Drinking can serve as a "reset" for some (short) time, but the truth is that with regular abuse of this type of substance, depression very often develops and occurs in such a way that instead of solving this problem, it worsens even more.

Anxiety.

Alcohol is a very dangerous substance. a depressant that suppresses brain activity. When physical stress is combined with alcohol intake, it can lead to the development of anxiety disorder, which is manifested by feelings of General anxiety, nightmares at night and negative thoughts.

Personality change.

Alcoholism can make a big difference human's. Often, people who are calm and balanced when they are sober become aggressive and selfish after they start drinking too much. They instantly change their mood, and in the moments of alcohol overdose, they can verbally (and not only) hurt their loved ones, and then do not even remember about it this.

Denial of the problem.

Denial is quite common in alcoholics. They deny the existence of the problem and convince their relatives of this, so that they can continue to drink quietly further. People with this problem often do not realize how serious it is, and rarely seek help even from loved ones who do not not to mention the doctors.

Memory loss.

In alcoholism, there is an accumulation effect, as a result of which brain cells are damaged, some of them die, and nerve connections are disrupted. Under the influence of these disorders, problems with the brain begin to be observed, which uses difficult to remember new information, often they don't remember what they did the day before. In the end, a heavy drinker has mental disorders and amnesia.
